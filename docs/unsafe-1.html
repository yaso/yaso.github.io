It has been two years since I stepped on a book of Christopher Alexander called Community and privacy: towards a New Architecture of Humanism." I remember that I was in a library alone, outside my country, traveling because of work; and It was Sunday, after lunch.

We just had one of our face-to-face meetings of W3C Data on the Web Working Group, and my head was spinning around with the RDF data model, among billions of thoughts beyond transparency and access to knowledge, two of the main concepts involved in this group's work. First of all, I couldn't help noticing that the concepts of linked data are really the most precise abstract model of representation of knowledge that we have to express human interaction in screens today. The web is a brilliant expression of this model. Second, it is impressive how this model has been incorporated to the main spaces that we exist on the Web.
